# Exercise 1: Executive Onramp

**Duration:** ~90 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Beginner to Intermediate  

---

## Objective

Get from zero to practical value with Claude Code in one session.

This exercise is designed for senior professionals who are:

- Interested in generative AI and tools like Claude Code
- Strong in business judgment but not hands-on with coding
- Looking for immediate, client-ready outcomes

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

1. Produced high-quality outputs using copy/paste prompts
2. Learned a repeatable prompt framework for executive work
3. Practiced iterative refinement and critical challenge
4. Built a personal "prompt playbook" for client delivery

---

## Exercise Design: Two Blocks

- **Block A — Copy/Paste Wins:** You copy prompts exactly, get strong results, and see what good looks like.
- **Block B — Guided Thinking:** We remove scaffolding step by step so you decide what to ask and how to steer output quality.

---

## Block A: Copy/Paste Wins

### Step A1: Board-ready summary (~10 min)

**Goal:** Generate a concise board briefing from source files.

**Prompt to copy/paste:**

```text
Read company-context/COMPANY.md, company-context/MARKET.md, and company-context/PRODUCTS.md.
Create a one-page board briefing for MIG's executive committee with:
1) Current position (where MIG stands now)
2) Top 3 opportunities in the next 12 to 18 months
3) Top 3 risks in the next 12 to 18 months
4) 5 concrete actions for the next quarter

Constraints:
- Maximum 300 words
- Use bullet points
- Include specific numbers where available
- Keep language direct and non-technical
```

**What to look for:**

- Clear structure and concise language
- Specific facts and numbers
- Action-oriented recommendations

<details>
<summary>Example output</summary>

**MIG Executive Committee — Board Briefing**

**Current Position**
- EUR 1,8B gross written premium (GWP), ~2.500 employees across 4 markets
- Combined ratio: 96,2% — profitable but with limited margin for deterioration
- Solvency II ratio: 178% — well-capitalized, capacity for growth investment
- LusoProtect acquisition (Portugal, 2024) adds EUR 95M GWP and ASF-licensed platform
- Project Tramuntana digital transformation 60% complete (EUR 57M of EUR 95M deployed)

**Top 3 Opportunities (12-18 months)**
1. **Portugal expansion via LusoProtect** — SME commercial property segment is underserved (32% penetration in rural areas). Target: EUR 113M Portugal GWP by end of 2026.
2. **Digital distribution acceleration** — Portuguese market is ~10% digital vs. 20% EU average. First-mover advantage in digital motor and home products.
3. **Reinsurance cost optimization** — Combined MIG+LusoProtect portfolio (EUR 1,9B) enables renegotiation of facultative treaties. Estimated saving: EUR 3-4M annually.

**Top 3 Risks (12-18 months)**
1. **Integration execution risk** — SiGeSeg platform migration to Guidewire on critical path. Delay beyond Q4 2027 blocks commercial product launches.
2. **Motor portfolio deterioration in Spain** — Andalusia loss ratio at 91,1%, well above 70% target. If unchecked, adds ~EUR 12M to annual incurred claims.
3. **Regulatory divergence** — ASF (Portugal) requirements for Solvency II reporting differ from DGSFP (Spain). Compliance setup requires EUR 1,2M investment.

**5 Actions for Next Quarter**
1. Approve Year 1 Portugal investment case (EUR 9,5M) at April board meeting
2. Launch Earnix pricing deployment for LusoProtect motor book — target go-live Q3 2026
3. Commission independent actuarial review of Andalusia motor portfolio
4. Begin broker partnership outreach in Lisbon and Porto for commercial property
5. Complete Project Tramuntana Phase 3 milestone review (digital claims FNOL)

</details>

### Step A2: Turn insight into a 3-slide storyline (~10 min)

**Goal:** Move from analysis to client communication.

**Prompt to copy/paste:**

```text
Using the previous board briefing, create a 3-slide storyline for a Partner update.

Format:
- Slide 1: Situation and why it matters now
- Slide 2: What is changing in the market and where MIG should play
- Slide 3: Recommended decisions and next 90-day plan

For each slide provide:
- Slide title
- 3 to 4 bullets
- Speaker notes (2 to 3 lines)
```

**What to look for:**

- Tight narrative flow across slides
- Business language suitable for clients and steering committees
- Clear "so what" on every slide

<details>
<summary>Example output</summary>

**Slide 1: MIG is well-positioned but faces a critical growth window**

- EUR 1,8B GWP with 178% solvency ratio — strong capital base for investment
- Core Spanish market growing at 4,2% — solid but decelerating
- LusoProtect acquisition gives us a licensed platform in the fastest-growing market in our footprint (Portugal: 6,9% CAGR)
- Window of opportunity: Portuguese SME commercial market is underserved before larger players invest

*Speaker notes: Frame the urgency. We have 12-18 months before Fidelidade or Ageas move into the SME commercial gap. Our capital position allows us to invest without compromising solvency.*

**Slide 2: Portugal is the growth engine — three segments to target**

- **SME commercial property:** 32% penetration in rural areas, EUR 660M addressable market, minimal dedicated competition
- **Motor expansion:** Deploy Earnix pricing to improve LusoProtect's 6,7% share — target 8,5% by 2028
- **Digital home insurance:** Portuguese market 10% digital vs. 20% EU average — first-mover advantage for urban renters

*Speaker notes: The scorecard analysis ranks these three segments highest on both attractiveness and feasibility. They build on capabilities MIG already has in Spain.*

**Slide 3: Invest EUR 25,5M over 3 years — breakeven by Year 3**

- Year 1 (2026): EUR 9,5M investment → launch commercial property + Earnix pricing (target EUR 113M GWP)
- Year 2 (2027): EUR 10M investment → scale motor + launch home (target EUR 153M GWP)
- Year 3 (2028): EUR 6M investment → combined ratio below 97% (target EUR 198M GWP)
- **Decision needed:** Approve Year 1 investment at April board; appoint Portugal integration lead by May

*Speaker notes: The EUR 25,5M total is ~1,4% of current GWP — manageable within existing capital buffers. Conservative scenario still reaches EUR 165M GWP by 2028.*

</details>

### Step A3: Draft a senior stakeholder email (~10 min)

**Goal:** Convert the storyline into executive communication.

**Prompt to copy/paste:**

```text
Draft an email from a Managing Director to a client CEO.

Context:
- Purpose: align on priority decisions before next steering committee
- Tone: confident, concise, practical
- Length: 180 to 220 words

Include:
1) A sharp opening with context
2) Three recommendations (business-focused)
3) A clear ask for the upcoming meeting
4) Subject line options (3)
```

**What to look for:**

- Credible executive tone
- No generic filler
- Explicit decision asks

<details>
<summary>Example output</summary>

**Subject line options:**
1. MIG Portugal — Three priorities for your review ahead of April steering committee
2. Steering committee prep: Portugal growth plan — decisions needed
3. MIG x LusoProtect: recommended next steps for April

**Email:**

Dear Ms. Torres,

Following our analysis of MIG's Portugal expansion opportunity, I wanted to share three priority recommendations ahead of the April steering committee.

**1. Approve Year 1 investment (EUR 9,5M).** The LusoProtect platform gives us a licensed, operational base in a market growing at 6,9% annually. The SME commercial property segment is materially underserved — 32% penetration in rural areas versus 58% in Spain. Delaying beyond Q2 risks losing first-mover advantage.

**2. Deploy Earnix pricing for the motor book immediately.** LusoProtect's motor portfolio (EUR 68M GWP) is priced using manual tariffs. Migrating to GLM-based pricing would improve loss ratios by an estimated 3-5 percentage points within 12 months — a EUR 2-3,4M annual impact.

**3. Appoint a Portugal integration lead by May.** Execution risk is the primary concern. A dedicated senior leader with dual reporting to Barcelona and Lisbon will be critical for the SiGeSeg migration and broker partnership buildout.

I have prepared a detailed briefing pack covering the market analysis, competitive landscape, and financial projections. I would welcome 30 minutes before the steering committee to walk through the key assumptions and address any questions.

Best regards

### Step A4: Stress-test the recommendation (~10 min)

**Goal:** Use Claude Code as a thinking challenger, not only a drafting tool.

**Prompt to copy/paste:**

```text
Challenge the recommendations you just gave me.

Provide:
1) The 5 strongest objections a skeptical CFO or COO could raise
2) For each objection: why it is valid, what evidence is missing, and how to address it
3) A revised recommendation set that is more robust

Be direct. Avoid polite filler.
```

**What to look for:**

- Quality of counterarguments
- Evidence gaps identified clearly
- Improved recommendations after challenge

<details>
<summary>Example output</summary>

**5 Objections a Skeptical CFO/COO Would Raise**

**1. "EUR 25,5M is a lot to bet on a market we don't know."**
- *Why it's valid:* MIG has zero track record in Portugal. The EUR 9,5M Year 1 ask assumes we can build broker relationships and launch commercial products simultaneously — both are unproven.
- *Evidence missing:* No pilot data. No broker letters of intent. No comparable precedent from MIG's history of entering new markets.
- *How to address:* Stage the investment with explicit go/no-go gates. Commit EUR 4M for H1 2026 (Earnix + team hiring); release the remaining EUR 5,5M only after Q2 broker pipeline review.

**2. "Why not just fix Andalusia first? That's EUR 12M in excess losses sitting in our own backyard."**
- *Why it's valid:* Andalusia motor loss ratio at 91,1% is an immediate P&L drag. Fixing it requires no acquisition integration, no new regulatory relationships.
- *Evidence missing:* No comparison of risk-adjusted ROI between Andalusia remediation vs. Portugal growth.
- *How to address:* Run both in parallel. The Andalusia fix is an underwriting action (re-pricing, portfolio pruning) that doesn't compete for the same resources as Portugal expansion.

**3. "The EUR 198M GWP target by 2028 assumes 8,5% motor market share — that's a 27% increase in 3 years."**
- *Why it's valid:* Motor is the most competitive segment. Fidelidade alone holds ~30%. Growing share requires either price competition (margin destruction) or product differentiation (investment).
- *Evidence missing:* No customer acquisition cost estimates. No competitor response modelling.
- *How to address:* Model a conservative scenario at 7,5% share (EUR 165M GWP). The business case still works at breakeven by late Year 3.

**4. "Integration risk is being underestimated — the SiGeSeg migration is on the critical path."**
- *Why it's valid:* Platform migrations in insurance routinely run 6-12 months late. If SiGeSeg delays past Q4 2027, commercial product launches slip to 2028.
- *Evidence missing:* No independent technical assessment of SiGeSeg migration complexity.
- *How to address:* Commission a 4-week technical due diligence before committing Year 2 budget. Build a parallel launch path using LusoProtect's existing platform for simple products.

**5. "We're spreading management attention too thin — Tramuntana is only 60% complete."**
- *Why it's valid:* Project Tramuntana has EUR 38M remaining spend. Adding Portugal creates competing demands on the same CTO, CFO, and operations teams.
- *Evidence missing:* No resource capacity analysis showing whether current teams can absorb Portugal workload.
- *How to address:* The Portugal integration lead (Recommendation #3) should have a dedicated team — not shared Tramuntana resources. Budget an additional EUR 1,2M for 4 Portugal-specific hires.

**Revised Recommendations**

1. **Stage the investment with gates** — approve EUR 4M for H1 2026, with a Q2 review before releasing the full Year 1 budget
2. **Run Andalusia remediation in parallel** — separate workstream, separate budget, separate owner
3. **Model conservative and base scenarios** — present both to the steering committee with explicit assumptions
4. **Commission SiGeSeg technical assessment** — 4 weeks, independent assessor, before Year 2 commitment
5. **Appoint a dedicated Portugal team** — do not load Tramuntana resources

</details>

## Block B: Guided Thinking

This block progressively removes scaffolding.

### Step B1: Fill-in prompt framework (~15 min)

**Goal:** Learn a reusable prompt structure for executive work.

Here is a template for structuring executive prompts. Your task is to fill in the `[BRACKETS]` yourself, then paste the completed prompt into Claude Code.

**The template:**

```text
You are my [ROLE].
Task: [WHAT YOU NEED]
Audience: [WHO WILL READ THIS]
Decision needed: [WHAT DECISION SHOULD BE MADE]
Context files: [FILES OR FACTS]
Constraints:
- Length: [X words]
- Tone: [TONE]
- Format: [TABLE / BULLETS / EMAIL / SLIDE OUTLINE]
- Must include: [NUMBERS, RISKS, ASSUMPTIONS, OPTIONS]

Before answering:
1) Ask up to 3 clarifying questions if needed
2) State assumptions
3) Then provide the deliverable
```

**What to do:**

1. Copy the template into a text editor
2. Fill in every `[BRACKET]` for this scenario: *a steering committee pre-read for MIG's Portugal growth plan*. Use the MIG context files for reference.
3. Paste your completed prompt into Claude Code and run it
4. Compare the output to what you got in Block A — is it more focused?

<details>
<summary>Example of a filled-in prompt</summary>

```text
You are my strategy advisor.
Task: Write a steering committee pre-read for MIG's Portugal expansion
Audience: CEO and 4 regional directors (non-technical, time-poor)
Decision needed: Whether to approve EUR 25M investment over 3 years
Context files: company-context/COMPANY.md, company-context/MARKET.md
Constraints:
- Length: 400 words max
- Tone: Confident but balanced, flag uncertainties clearly
- Format: Bullet points with a summary table of key metrics
- Must include: GWP targets, investment required, breakeven timeline, top 3 risks

Before answering:
1) Ask up to 3 clarifying questions if needed
2) State assumptions
3) Then provide the deliverable
```

</details>

### Step B2: Improve a weak prompt (~15 min)

**Goal:** Build prompt quality judgment.

Start with this weak prompt:

```text
Can you analyze MIG and tell me what to do in Portugal?
```

Your task:

1. Rewrite it into a high-quality executive prompt
2. Explain why your rewritten version is better
3. Run your rewritten prompt

**Quality checklist:**

- Specific objective
- Explicit audience
- Decision framing
- Output format constraints
- Data/context references

---

### Step B3: Multi-turn refinement (~15 min)

**Goal:** Learn how to iterate in short, high-leverage turns.

Round sequence:

1. Ask for first output  
2. Critique it using this exact structure:  
   - "Keep:"  
   - "Change:"  
   - "Remove:"  
   - "Add:"  
3. Ask for revision with tighter constraints

**Prompt to run:**

```text
Create a decision memo for a Partner meeting on MIG's strategic priorities.
Maximum 400 words.
```

Then refine with:

```text
Revise using this feedback:
Keep: [what worked]
Change: [what needs adjustment]
Remove: [what is unnecessary]
Add: [what is missing]

Constraints:
- Keep under 300 words
- Include 3 quantified assumptions
- End with a clear decision recommendation
```

### Step B4: Open challenge (~20 min)

**Goal:** Apply everything with minimal scaffolding.

Scenario:

You have a client steering committee tomorrow. You need a practical briefing pack in 20 minutes.

Your task:

Create your own prompt (no template provided) that asks Claude Code to produce:

- A one-page executive brief
- A risk register (top 8 risks)
- A 90-day action plan with owners

Constraints:

- Must be readable by non-technical executives
- Must include specific numbers from available context
- Must distinguish facts vs assumptions

## Self-Check Rubric

Use this rubric to assess the quality of your prompts and outputs. Score each dimension from 0 to 10 (total score: 40):

| Dimension | What "10" Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Clarity of ask | Prompt is precise, scoped, and unambiguous |
| Business relevance | Output is decision-oriented, not generic |
| Actionability | Clear next steps, owners, and timeline |
| Critical thinking | Risks, assumptions, and trade-offs are explicit |

Suggested interpretation:

- 32-40: Strong executive operator
- 24-31: Good foundation, improve prompt precision
- Below 24: Repeat Block B with tighter constraints

---

## Common Pitfalls (And Fixes)

1. **Prompt too broad**
- Fix: add audience, decision, format, and word limit.

2. **Output sounds generic**
- Fix: require specific numbers and named assumptions.

3. **No decision recommendation**
- Fix: explicitly ask for a recommendation and rationale.

4. **Too much text, low signal**
- Fix: request bullets, max length, and a prioritized top-5.

5. **No challenge of first draft**
- Fix: always add one "challenge this recommendation" turn.
